01
★ Oral   Agentic RL · Tool Use

AgentFlow: In-The-Flow Agentic System Optimization for Effective Planning and Tool Use

Z. Li, H. Zhang, S. Han, S. Liu, J. Xie, Y. Zhang, Y. Choi, J. Zou, P. Lu  ·  Stanford, NVIDIA

Instead of training one monolithic policy that interleaves thoughts and tool calls, AgentFlow decomposes work across four modules (planner, executor, verifier, generator) coordinated through evolving memory. The planner is optimized in-the-flow via Flow-GRPO, which converts long-horizon multi-turn RL into tractable single-turn updates by broadcasting trajectory-level outcomes to every turn. At 7B scale, beats GPT-4o by +14.9% on search, +14.0% on agentic, +14.5% on math benchmarks.

Why it matters
The credit-assignment trick — broadcast a trajectory-level signal to every step — is general. Expect to see it adapted to other multi-turn agent training pipelines within the year.

02
Oral  ·  Generative Policies · Robotics

Mean Flow Policy with Instantaneous Velocity Constraint for One-Step Action Generation

G. Zhan, L. Tao, P. Wang, Y. Wang, Y. Chen, Y. Li, H. Li, M. Tomizuka, S. Li  ·  Tsinghua, UC Berkeley

Flow-based policies trade expressiveness for inference cost via the number of flow steps. MFP learns a mean velocity field for one-step action generation, with an instantaneous velocity constraint that acts as a boundary condition during training. Theory shows this preserves expressiveness; experiments show SOTA on Robomimic and OGBench with substantial speedups over diffusion and multi-step flow baselines.

Why it matters
Closes the inference gap between flow-based and unimodal policies. Real-time robotics no longer has to choose between expressiveness and latency.

03
Oral  ·  Continual RL · Plasticity

FIRE: Frobenius-Isometry Reinitialization for Balancing the Stability–Plasticity Tradeoff

I. Han, S. Park, S. Oh, D. Kim, H. Lee, K. Kim  ·  GIST

Networks trained on nonstationary data must trade off retaining knowledge (stability) against adapting to new tasks (plasticity). FIRE reinitializes weights by minimizing Frobenius distance to past weights subject to an isometry constraint (Newton–Schulz approximation). Evaluated on SAC for HumanoidBench and DQN for Atari — consistently outperforms both naive training and standard reinitialization schemes.

Why it matters
Plasticity loss is the silent killer of long-horizon RL runs. FIRE makes the fix mathematically principled rather than a tuning knob.

04
Oral  ·  Long Context · Memory

MemAgent: Reshaping Long-Context LLM with Multi-Conv RL-Based Memory Agent

H. Yu, T. Chen, J. Feng, J. Chen, W. Dai, Q. Yu, Y.-Q. Zhang, W.-Y. Ma, J. Liu, M. Wang, H. Zhou  ·  ByteDance, Tsinghua

Processes long documents in segments while updating an overwrite-style memory; trained end-to-end by extending DAPO for independent-context multi-conversation generation. Extrapolates from an 8K training context to a 3.5M-token QA task with under 10% performance loss, and scores over 95% on the 512K NIAH benchmark.

Why it matters
A different angle on the long-context problem: don't extend attention, learn to summarize with RL. The 437× extrapolation factor is the loudest number in this issue.

05
Oral  ·  Environments · Biomedical Agents

MedAgentGym: A Scalable Agentic Training Environment for Code-Centric Reasoning in Biomedical Data Science

R. Xu, Y. Zhuang, Y. Zhong, Y. Yu et al.  ·  Emory, Google

An interactive training environment for biomedical coding agents: 72,413 task instances across 129 categories in executable sandboxes with verifiable ground truth. Training Med-Copilot on it yields +43.02% from offline RL and +45.28% from online RL, reaching parity with GPT-4o while remaining open-source and privacy-preserving.

Why it matters
High-fidelity training environments are the new gating resource for agentic RL. MedAgentGym shows the recipe: real-world tasks, executable verification, open-source release.

Oral  ·  Offline RL · Robotics

Cross-Embodiment Offline RL for Heterogeneous Robot Datasets

H. Abe, T. Osa, Y. Mukuta, T. Harada  ·  University of Tokyo, RIKEN AIP

Unites offline RL with cross-embodiment learning to escape the per-platform demonstration bottleneck for robot pretraining. The systematic analysis spans a new locomotion suite of 16 distinct robot platforms, and shows the combined approach decisively beats pure behavior cloning when the data is rich in suboptimal trajectories.

Why it matters
The robotics community has been BC-by-default for years; this is principled evidence that suboptimal data is an asset, not noise — if you use the right algorithm.
